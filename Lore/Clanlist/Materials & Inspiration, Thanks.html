What Is DXMP?
3
4
Teased already in late 90s, multi-player deathmatch format trending in the gaming scene at the time was seemingly an afterthought for Deus Ex, seen more as an additional sales point for the title and apparently to be added if time and funds permit.
5
After the successful launch in June 23, 2000 which saw good sales in North America and turned blockbuster in Europe, a team was hired to create the competitive online experience and sneak it to the player with one of the December patches.
6
This means that players who owned early release of Deus Ex would not necessarily even be aware that it had a multiplayer, although it would eventually ship with the enticing GOTY version.
7
While criticized by at least one outlet as looking outdated already at game launch, the engine for Deus Ex and DXMP was built for online deathmatch, yet somehow it managed to launch in a state where players can modify weapon values from chat.
8
In principle that same issue persists to this very day today.
9
The game also didn't run very well, pushing limits of hardware and probably the engine: there is an amount of lag we can ignore in our immersive sim, but it is harder to turn a blind eye to crashes and single digit frames when score is on the line.
10
All of these factors led to a small community with a highly charged environment, every bit as dramatic as it was competitive.
11
With Warren Spector's encouragement there were a few drastically different game modes developed by the modding community, but nothing fueled the conflicts like the rise of CTDM servers with augs set to 0 and skills set to max.
12
This list of clans, the communities linked here, they all tell a story of players that lived this chaos, many becoming very attached to the unique game or perhaps even to the community; in principle, the concept of DXMP had promise.
13
It is worth a thought if we are indeed not talking about the grandfather of modern tactical hero shooters, the one you didn't know existed.

Considerations
26
27
Clan list is focused on showcasing and telling the story of various Tags without much discrimination regarding its success, size, longevity, date and so on.
28
Some of the strongest clans in this game lived mere days or weeks, and some of the longest-living clans were not very strong; I think both are important parameters, and I also don't want to neglect tags that represent other types of groups in-game.
29
Sorting and perhaps some filter views will allow get the specific information you are after (early clans, large clans, wars and rosters etc) but due to gaps in list you might filter out an important clan until list is filled in enough.
30
First tags were added from known clans, listed clans, while unknown tags were not added; last additions to this list come from unknown tags in screenshots and the work is still ongoing.
31
You can assume that significant clans by now all have made it into the list, but probably many dozens of short-lived tags are missing.
32
Rosters and clan wars are currently incredibly incomplete and sorting by them is more of a novelty rather than good statistics - hopefully over time those fields are filled in better.
33
Game types field is very sporadically filled and largely from an experienced guess, while I could also randomly guess the other clans depending on their era I prefer not to do so until I have better evidence of their strength.
